Site order

The structure follows the resident's actual decision sequence

1SafetyEmergency triage, shelter, utilities, unsafe access.
2LocationCity/address, rain, elevation, FEMA zones, GIS, drains.
3CauseFloodwater, sewer backup, pipe leak, roof opening, seepage, mixed source.
4EvidencePhotos, videos, receipts, reports, item list, water line, timeline.
5SupportInsurance, aid, housing, utilities, food, childcare, repairs.
6RebuildImmediate fixes, permits, substantial damage, resilience upgrades.
7PacketZIP, print, forward summary, source links, open questions.

Plain language system

Writing rules used across the platform

RuleWhyExample in the product
Start with the user's situationFlood users may be scared, displaced, or overloaded."I might be unsafe" before "Insurance" or "Packet."
Use verbs before nounsAction language is easier than agency language."Build claim packet" instead of "Documentation resources."
Pair action with proofRecovery depends on evidence.Every guide section says what to do and what to save.
Separate similar needsSimilar labels can send users to the wrong place.Electric/gas assistance is separate from water/sewer/plumbing help.
Localize sourcesMichigan has many local jurisdictions and providers.Macomb is labeled as a local profile, not the statewide default.
Avoid false certaintyInsurance, aid, permits, and code decisions are official determinations."Likely route" and "confirm with insurer/local official."

Hope and trust

How the platform earns trust

Message hierarchy

  • Immediate promise: know what to do next.
  • Proof: official sources and local-source replacement pages.
  • Value: one packet for insurance, aid, repairs, and follow-up.
  • Emotional benefit: less panic, fewer missed documents, clearer recovery path.

Conversion paths

  • Emergency user: safety steps to housing.
  • Insurance user: cause classifier to packet.
  • Aid user: need matcher to local source page.
  • Repair user: immediate-fix playbook to permit/rebuild guidance.
  • Helper: source pages to local next steps.

Content model

Reusable pattern for every page

1SituationWhat is happening and why the page matters.
2ActionWhat the resident should do next.
3ProofWhat documents, photos, receipts, or records are needed.
4SourceWhich local, state, federal, or professional source confirms the next step.

Important: This page explains the platform's mission. For urgent needs, start with the emergency page, resident guide, assistance page, or packet builder.
