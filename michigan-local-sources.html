Michigan source system

Every town needs its own local source page.

Macomb County is one local context. A resident in another Michigan city, village, township, or county needs the same guidance structure, but with their own emergency office, utility provider, community action agency, building department, floodplain administrator, GIS, and drain maps.

Rule Statewide facts plus local contacts.

FEMA, EGLE, MDHHS, DIFS, CDC, EPA, and MI 211 provide statewide guidance. Cities, townships, counties, utilities, and local agencies provide the contact points.

Local example Macomb stays as one profile.

Macomb Community Action, Macomb Emergency Management, Macomb GIS, and county drain maps remain useful, but they should not be the default for all Michigan users.

Local profile template

What each Michigan town or county source page needs

Create one local profile per resident location. The platform should ask for city/township, county, address, utility providers, and floodplain/building jurisdiction before showing local source links.

Dedicated source pages

Separate the source page by user need.

Residents should not sort through a giant source list during a flood. Each page should answer one source question.

Water, energy, food, housing, childcare, repairs Assistance source page Use for: finding the correct statewide and local aid entry point. Local changes: community action agency, utility provider, county/city programs. Rain, elevation, GIS, drains, flood maps Map and data source page Use for: proving location context and local flood factors. Local changes: county GIS, drain maps, public works, parcel data. Permits, inspectors, floodplain officials Building source page Use for: knowing who decides permits and floodplain requirements. Local changes: city/township building department and floodplain administrator. Flood, homeowners, renters, auto, claims Insurance source page Use for: understanding policy routes and consumer-help sources. Local changes: insurer, agent, adjuster, local disaster declaration status.
