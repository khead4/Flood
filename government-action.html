Local law questions

Ask these before repair, rebuilding, buying, or challenging a decision.

QuestionWhy it mattersEvidence to bring
What municipality controls zoning and permits for this parcel?The city or township controls local zoning, permits, and many appeal routes.Address, parcel number, municipality, tax record, photos.
Is the parcel in a mapped flood zone, floodway, drain corridor, wetland, shoreline, or easement?These can change what can be repaired, filled, rebuilt, or appealed.FEMA map, Macomb GIS screenshot, drain map, survey, site plan.
Does the work trigger permits, inspections, substantial damage, substantial improvement, or elevation certificate review?Residents can spend money too early if they do not know the approval gates.Repair estimate, damage estimate, contractor scope, photos, prior permits.
Is the problem caused by private property conditions, public drainage, sewer capacity, road runoff, or a combination?Different offices handle private repairs, public drains, utility backups, and zoning changes.Rain dates, photos, videos, plumber report, public works notes, neighbor statements.
What standard would prevent repeat damage rather than only meet the minimum code?Minimum compliance may not protect against heavier rain, low elevation, sewer backup, or repeated flooding.Elevation, water line, utility height, drain route, proposed mitigation plan.

Code gaps

Use the experience report to show where current rules are not enough.

Gap 1Flood maps do not capture every flooding mechanism.Rain intensity, sewer backup, low road grades, impervious surfaces, drains, and localized ponding can harm properties outside mapped flood zones.
Gap 2Permits are too hard to understand during recovery.Residents need one clear path showing when demolition, electrical, HVAC, plumbing, structural repair, grading, or utility relocation needs approval.
Gap 3Minimum code is not the same as flood-ready housing.A stronger standard raises utilities, improves drainage, uses flood-resistant lower materials, adds backflow protection, and plans for heavier rainfall.
Gap 4Public drainage and private building risk are separated.A home may meet building rules and still flood because the street, sewer, ditch, drain, or runoff path cannot handle the water.
